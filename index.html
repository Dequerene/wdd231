About Me

My name is Osvaldo Dequerene João, and I'm a software development student from Mozambique currently studying in Utah. I'm enrolled in BYU Pathway Worldwide, where I'm building a strong foundation in web development, databases, and programming. I've worked on multiple academic projects using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, and SQL, and I'm passionate about turning ideas into real digital products. I also served a two-year volunteer mission in Angola, where I worked as a Technology and Finance Secretary, supporting mission offices with troubleshooting, device setup, and organization of financial records. That experience strengthened my technical skills, discipline, and attention to detail. I'm a fast learner, adaptable, and motivated by challenges. My long-term goal is to become a professional software engineer and entrepreneur, creating opportunities for young IT talents and helping build a strong tech ecosystem in Mozambique.
